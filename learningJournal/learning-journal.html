Learning Journal

Exploring Forms & User Experience

What I Learned

From the reading ( Best Practices for Form Design)

Good form design focuses on clarity, simplicity, and guiding the user. Forms should reduce effort by minimizing required input and using helpful tools like dropdowns or autofill. Clear labels are important because users should always understand what information is being asked.

Observations

I have noticed that long forms feel overwhelming and make me lose interest quickly. Forms that break information into steps feel easier to complete. I also find it frustrating when error messages are unclear or appear too late.

Form Evaluation

I evaluated the UC Davis Advising Appointment System (Here).

This system is important because students rely on it to schedule academic advising, so it needs to be clear and easy to use.

One thing that works well is that the form is structured step-by-step, which helps guide the user through the process. It reduces confusion by not showing everything at once. The categories for appointment types are also helpful because they organize different advising needs.

Another detail I liked was the moving buttons/ drop down menu They give subtle feedback and make the interface feel more interactive and responsive, which improves the overall user experience.
